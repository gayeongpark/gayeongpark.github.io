# First Journal

## Background

During my undergraduate studies, I was driven by a deep curiosity about the world around me. Although my major was Fine Art, I intentionally broadened my academic pursuits by enrolling in courses beyond the arts. My semester schedules included subjects like international affairs, history, English, and applied mathematics, some of which were taught by international professors. I just found it inspiring to hear diverse perspectives from them. At the time, I felt a need to enhance my critical thinking skills and sought exposure to fields I had never explored, believing that a multidisciplinary approach would enrich my artistic perspective.

One of the most formative experiences was a module titled “Global Issues in English.” This class provided an opportunity to engage in interesting discussions about cultural topics such as Spain's siesta tradition, Australia's summer Christmas, and the phenomenon of digital obsession. The collaborative debates with English-major students and a professor from the UK sparked my interest in international affairs. Because I found it fascinating how individuals could express their own perspectives, challenge opposing views, embrace alternative opinions, and ultimately reach thoughtful conclusions in English. While each topic was explored at an introductory level, the experience ignited my desire to understand global issues more deeply. To pursue this interest further, I applied for the Diplomacy Workshop for Undergraduate Students at the Korea National Diplomatic Academy. This experience was transformative. I had the chance to connect with brilliant peers and Korean diplomats, participate in realistic UN-style debates, and gain insights into the role of diplomats in addressing global challenges.

Building on this momentum, I luckily joined a project with the Korea International Cooperation Agency (KOICA), where I contributed to naming a new KOICA facility. After this project, I could open the door to an internship at a state office, where I experienced first-hand the role of public administration for artists. Inspired by this experience, I later joined the International Rice Research Institute (IRRI) as a Senior Assistant Administrative Coordinator. In this role, I served as a liaison between the agriculture department of Korean government and IRRI, supporting administrative operations and facilitating effective communication. I had the privilege of attending discussions led by top agriculture researchers, recording meeting minutes, and managing financial processes, such as general ledger preparation and inventory management.

While I gained valuable insight into the inner workings of global research and cooperation, I found myself yearning for a more active role — one where I could not only support operations but also contribute my own ideas and see them come to fruition in diverse projects. However, the rigid hierarchy within international organizations made career advancement challenging, limiting opportunities for growth. This realization prompted me to seek new challenges. After more than three years of experience in administrative coordination, I decided to step away from my role to reorient my career. My goal was to become someone who not only facilitates but also leads initiatives that create a visible, positive impact on the world.
